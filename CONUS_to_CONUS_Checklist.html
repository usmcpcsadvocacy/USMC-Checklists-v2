I. First Steps After Receiving Orders

These are the actions that should be initiated immediately due to longer processing time, or because they are an early investment into the overall project. Most action items will happen simultaneously; some take time, so initiate and then move to the next step.

As soon as you receive your orders, verify all details are accurate:

  • Check service member information, duty stations (present/future commands), family member details (names and birth dates), and reporting dates
  • Notify your supporting admin office (S1/IPAC) of any errors
  • Ensure dependents are specifically listed on your orders. If not, submit a formal request for "Accompanied Orders" through your chain of command
  • Ensure all endorsements with all funding lines of accounting are included

Tips:

If you have a short-fuse shipment, contact your local DMO immediately.
If you or a family member is enrolled in EFMP, initiate the Ref 1 EFMP checklist ASAP.

Create a rough timeline for your move:

  • Check the NLT (No Later Than) date on your orders and work backwards
  • Outline your preferred pack-out/pick-up dates, route, or flight dates along with alternative dates
  • Use one calendar as a central location for all PCS-related items
  • If you opted for a PPM, determine how much lead time you need to pack; or if you will hire packers
  • If you've opted for a GPM, you must decide on a "7-day scheduling window" in which the TSP will arrange to pick up your shipment
  • Your "Move Date" is your "Pick Up Date" — the date you would ideally like everything loaded. Choose a 7-day window. Enter the last day into DPS and the window will auto-populate

Example: Move Date = Apr 24 → DPS auto-populates Apr 18–24. TSP choosing Apr 18 pickup means pack days may be Apr 13, 14 & 17.

Check out the Ref 23 7-Day Scheduling Window Fact Sheet.
Once your shipment is awarded in DPS, the TSP must contact you within 3 days to confirm a pick-up date.
For parents of school-age children: Confirm early withdrawal/late enrollment dates with current and prospective schools when choosing move dates.

Apply for base housing even if you intend to live off base. Use the Ref 5 Installation Search to find your local installation housing page.

Search for your local MCCS Information, Referral, & Relocation (IR&R) website to sign up:

  • Per Ref 3 MCO 1754.10B, a PCS workshop is required for the service member and highly encouraged for the spouse
  • Visit the Ref 4 IR&R page to find your local office
  • A great resource if you are unable to attend: Ref 22 NAVSUP Counseling Webinar Videos

Sign into your Marine On-Line (MOL) account to complete your Outbound Interview (OBI):

  • All Marines in receipt of PCS orders will utilize the OBI module within MOL
  • This covers all information needed by IPAC/admin to finalize PCS orders and will determine your travel entitlements

Contact the Sponsorship Coordinator (SC) of your gaining command or your local IR&R Office:

  • Per Ref 2 MCO 1320.11H, sponsors are to be of equal or higher pay-grade to arriving service members
  • Commands are encouraged to match marital status
  • Your sponsor should be in contact with you no less than 60 days prior to arrival and will assist with housing, local resources, and more
  • Your sponsor should receive Sponsorship Training (IR&R Office) in order to assist you

II. Initial Helpful Resources

Visit these resources for PCS information and support:

USMC PCS Support Facebook group (CONUS) U.S. Marine Corps Passenger Transportation Facebook Page MCICOM G4 PCS Support Website 2026 Peak Season MARADMIN Personal Property Activity (PPA) - PCS 101 Complete Moving Guide

Organize all your important documents in one place:

  • Military: copies of orders, detaching endorsement, etc.
  • Personal: Social Security cards, medical/immunization/dental records, birth/marriage certificates, school records/transcripts/IEPs
  • Move paperwork: TSP inventory sheets, important receipts, vehicle storage paperwork, TLF (hotel) info, important contacts
  • Other: EFMP paperwork, college transcripts/licensure, naturalization certificates, divorce/adoption/guardianship/custody documents
Tip: Anything you would not want lost in the move should be hand-carried with you!

III. The Earlier, the Better (180–90 Days Out)

These are recommended timelines but can occur sooner/later based on other steps in the process.

It is crucial the service member understands the total weight of HHG they are authorized:

  • Entitlements can be verified in the DTR IV "It's Your Move" Ref 27 on page 5
  • Use the Ref 25 PPA weight estimator tool
  • Pro Gear does not count against your total weight allowance. Net weight limit is 2,000 lbs
  • Marines may request an additional 500 lbs for Spouse Pro Gear, which includes items required for employment and homeschool materials
  • All Pro Gear requests MUST be submitted to the local PPO BEFORE the move
  • Gun safes (<500 lbs) are authorized to ship in addition to your authorized weight, not to exceed 18,000 lbs. Must be shipped empty

Note: O6 and above must request additional weight allowance from their local TO/DMO.

  • Full PPM: You control your move and are eligible for 100% of the Government Constructed Cost (GCC)
  • GPM: The government handles packing, loading, shipping, unloading, and unpacking. The member is responsible for understanding their responsibilities found in the DTR Ref 27. (JTR 051501 Ref 8)
  • Partial PPM: Ship the majority via GPM and privately transport higher-value items
  • Small Package PPM: Ship smaller necessity items in an expedited manner. See the Ref 40 USMC Small Package PPM handout (JTR 051503 Ref 8)

See Attachments A and/or B at the end of this checklist based on your chosen shipment method.

As soon as you receive your orders, start with DMO for HHG applications to lock in pack-out and pick-up dates:

  • Use the Ref 5 Installation Search option to find your nearest DMO (or Personal Property Office if not on a USMC base)
  • If this is your first time moving, contact your local DMO first to receive instructions to create an account

NEW (April 2026): Homeschooling materials may now be included as PBP&E (Pro Gear) — see JTR Appendix A.

Log into Ref 26 DPS to schedule your shipments. Have the following ready:

  • Contact info, estimated weight, pick-up & delivery locations and dates (no weekends or holidays), and any special items/PBP&E (Pro Gear)
Tip: If you don't know your exact delivery address, use your new duty station (e.g., Camp Pendleton, CA) and update once you arrive.

If completing a PPM, you will select HHG then toggle "yes" when it prompts if you are conducting a PPM.

Completing this step will generate your DD Forms. Review first, then sign. Contact your local DMO with any questions.

Make a plan for your vehicles and dependents. Transportation options (JTR 050101 Ref 8):

  1. Government transportation
  2. Government-procured commercial transportation (e.g., flight)
  3. Personally procured commercial transportation (e.g., rental car)
  4. Privately owned transportation
  5. Mixed Mode transportation (more than one mode)
Tip: For families with 2 POVs, check JTR 052902 for "drive one, ship one" reimbursement options.

Discuss your plan with your local IPAC before making arrangements.

For trips over 400 miles, divide the official distance by 350 to determine authorized travel days. If the remainder is 51 or more, one additional travel day is allowed (JTR 050205 Ref 8).

Example: 2,400 miles ÷ 350 = 6 days + 300-mile remainder (300 > 51) = 7 travel days.

For unique dependent travel situations, visit JTR 0504.

Come up with a spending plan. There will be some up-front costs that will be reimbursed later, but also plan for start-up expenses once you arrive (pantry, cleaning supplies, utilities).

Do not forget to request Dislocation Allowance (DLA) on your travel voucher once your PCS is complete. Advanced DLA is no longer authorized if the active duty Marine has a GTCC.

Know your entitlements — call or visit your local IPAC:

  • TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense): Authorized 21 days for CONUS-to-CONUS moves. Can be split between losing and gaining PDS. Ref 9
  • DLA (Dislocation Allowance): Partially reimburses for relocation expenses (typically reimbursed upon check-in once travel claim is completed). Ref 10
  • Per Diem: Flat rate for each day of travel. SM may not receive both per diem and TLE for the same day. Ref 11
  • BAH: Kicks in once the service member checks in at the new duty station. Ref 12
  • GTCC: Know your limit. Ask your S-1/ODTA if you need to raise your limit. Ensure it is activated and in mission-critical status prior to departure
  • LICWO: Contact your chain of command regarding this entitlement. Ref 13

Decide what you will ship, hand carry, and what you will purge.

  • Set aside items not authorized to be moved or stored
  • See Appendix A-I "Hazardous Materials" Ref 28
  • Your TSP may have additional restrictions — discuss during your Pre-Move Survey

If you plan to maintain residency at your current PDS, ensure your DL is not due to expire. Check laws at both the new and old PDS.

Note: If using your driver's license to access a military installation or flying, a Ref 14 Real ID is required.

Dependents will need the active duty service member present to renew a military ID. Dependents without a Sponsor present must have a valid DD Form 1172-2 signed by the Sponsor and a Verifying Official (valid 90 days from signature).

  • Power of Attorney accepted if it states DEERS or Military ID card related transactions
  • See Ref 15 Military One Source for potentially renewing online

Check with base accommodations first and obtain a certificate of non-availability if applicable.

If you require a pet-friendly hotel, contact the hotel directly for policy and reservation.

Once you know your estimated moving dates, make a plan for your current home. Notify your landlord or make plans to sell/find renters if living in off-base housing.

Remember: The Ref 16 Servicemember Civil Relief Act may apply.

IV. The Earlier, The Better (90–30 Days Out)

Per JTR 050107 Ref 8, reimbursement is issued for relocation costs of ONE household pet (cat or dog). Moving within CONUS is limited to $550 for one pet per set of orders.

Request copies of pertinent immunization, dental, and other medical records — or request your doctor's office send them directly to your new MTF/PCM.

This process can take time, so notify providers well in advance.

Locate and contact the School Liaison for school registration information at your new duty station. Your current duty station can also provide assistance. Ref 17

CDCs often have waitlists. Find the CDC at your new duty station. Ref 18

  • Visit Ref 19 www.militarychildcare.com to add your child(ren) to a waitlist
  • Subsidies are available in some areas — visit Ref 20 Childcare Aware for more information
  • Be aware of the Pilot Program to Reimburse Child Care Provider Transportation Costs Due to a PCS (JTR 050108 Ref 8)

Information, Referral, & Relocation Offices (IR&R) Ref 4 have helpful resources about your new duty station on their websites.

V. The Last 30 Days Prior to Leaving Old Duty Station

These can be completed earlier but should be done prior to departure from old PDS.

Notify your credit card companies and banks that you are moving. Pay any outstanding bills or set up autopay while you are in transit to your new duty station.

It can take 7–10 business days to forward a mailing address through USPS. If you do not have a new address yet, consider placing a hold on your mail or forwarding to a family member temporarily.

Update your new address with healthcare providers and any other local services you utilize as soon as you have it so that outstanding bills can be forwarded directly to you.

Check any subscriptions, utilities, or delivery services you may have and cancel or update your address.

Check with your S1/Admin/ODTA to make sure your GTCC is activated and in "mission critical" status prior to travel.

Stay enrolled in your current TRICARE plan. You may need to transfer regions after you arrive. Fill or refill any prescriptions to carry you through your relocation period.

The active duty Marine will begin the check-out process typically within 30 days of departure. Ensure you ask any last-minute questions regarding entitlements during this time.

  • Consider blocking off a section of your home clearly labeled "DO NOT MOVE"
  • Set aside suitcases (for travel), trash, and donations
  • Keep passports, move binder, purses, etc. out of reach of movers — consider placing them in your car
  • Don't forget medications, along with refills to get you through the relocation process
  • Start setting aside snacks and entertainment, especially if traveling with children!

Clean your home and ensure none of your personal belongings are left behind. Complete your home inspection (if applicable), turn in the keys, and say goodbye to your neighbors!

VI. Travel to your New PDS

Keep copies of all receipts for gas, hotels, and food during your travel. Receipts may need to be submitted if utilizing the GTCC during travel days.

Weather can change in a moment's notice — track severe weather alerts along your route. Have a plan if severe weather hits and know where you should go.

VII. After Arriving to your New PDS

Your sponsor should assist with the check-in process and introduce you to your gaining command.

Ensure your Alert! profile is updated to reflect your new duty station. Ref 6

Update your TRICARE region if needed. This must be completed within 90 days as a qualifying life event. Learn more at Ref 21 Tricare Life Events.

If utilizing base housing, report to the family housing office for assignment.

This will update your pay and entitlements. Ensure you submit any reimbursable receipts (pet fees, TLE, etc.) and select yes to receiving Dislocation Allowance (DLA).

TLE may require a supplemental voucher depending on length of stay.

Check weekly on MOL travel tab for status updates on the travel voucher. Ensure all entitlements were paid at the correct rate.

Verify if your new duty station offers a welcome aboard / newcomers brief. Ref 7
