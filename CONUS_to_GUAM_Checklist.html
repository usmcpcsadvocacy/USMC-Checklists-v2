I. First Steps After Receiving Orders
(These are the actions that should be initiated immediately due to a longer processing time, or because it is an early investment into the overall project. Most of these actions will happen simultaneously, and if you rush to finalize too many plans too early)
Make sure your Orders are Accurate (Do this immediately)
As soon as you receive your orders, make sure all details are accurate.
  • Check service member information, duty stations (present/future commands), family member details (names and birth dates), and reporting dates. Let your supporting admin office (S1/IPAC) know of any errors.
  • Check to make sure dependents are specifically listed on your orders. If not, submit a formal request for "Accompanied Orders" through your chain of command.
  • Ensure all endorsements with all funding lines of accounting are included.
  • Tip: If you have a short-fuse shipment, contact your local DMO immediately.
  • Tip: If you or a family member is enrolled in EFMP, initiate the EFMP checklist ASAP. Ref 1
Grab a Calendar & Get Organized
Create a rough timeline for your move.
  • Check the NLT (No Later Than) date on your orders and work backwards. Outline your preferred pack-out/pick-up dates, route, or flight dates along with alternative dates. Use one calendar as a central location for all things PCS-related.
  • NOTE: The timeline for your shipments will vary depending on how early/late you choose to ship your belongings. There is no "right" answer to what order you send your shipment. For updates on weight and ballance being loaded, choose a 7-day window. Enter the last day into DPS and the window will auto-populate.
  • Pack Dates happen in the days immediately preceding your Pick Up Date — outside your 7-day spread but not on weekends or holidays without your permission.
  • Example: Move Date = Apr 24 → DPS auto-populates Apr 18-24. TSP choosing Apr 18 pickup means pack days may be Apr 13, 14 & 17.
  • Tip: Your shipment is awarded in DPS, the TSP must contact you within 3 days to confirm a pick-up date.
  • *For parents of school-age children: Confirm early withdrawal/late enrollment dates with current and prospective schools when choosing move dates.
Initiate Overseas Suitability Screening (OSS) process (Start immediately upon receipt of OCONUS orders)
Start the Suitability Screening process immediately upon receipt of orders by contacting your assigned Area Suitability/Overseas Screening Coordinator at the nearest Medical Treatment Facility (MTF).
  • The purpose of the OSS is to screen all family members of active duty and/or activated service members who require government-sponsored travel to OCONUS locations for potential travel concerns, which may include medical, educational, and/or dental needs, to assist with a more orderly execution of movement and to ensure appropriate assignment coordination.
  • All active duty Marines and activated reservists must meet with the Area Suitability/Overseas Screening Coordinator to meet the medical clearance requirements. Per BUMED Instruction 1300 2 B, the OSS process is required to be completed within 30 days for the service member(s) and 60 days for family member(s). Ref 43
  • NOTE: Area clearance is NOT required when PCSing to Guam.
  • For a guide on how to fill in the OSS forms, see the video from Camp Pendleton OSS office: Ref 44
  • General questions can be directed to the NEWLY established FMTS Cell. Ref 45
  • NOTE: You will want to collect medical / dental records to hand carry to your new duty station.
  • EFMP (Exceptional Family Member Program) families: Contact your local EFMP office upon receipt of orders to ensure all paperwork is current. Ask for a PCS Special Assignments Coordinator to meet the medical clearance requirements and discuss any concerns about the move. You can learn about EFMP family support services through Military OneSource. Ref 46
Sign up for an MCCS Relocation Class with your IR&R Office (if available)
Search for your local MCCS Information, Referral, & Relocation (IR&R) website to sign up.
  • Per MCO 1754.10B Ref 3, a PCS workshop is required for the active duty member and highly encouraged for the spouse. Visit the IR&R page Ref 4 to find your local office.
Submit your Outbound Interview (OBI)
Sign into your Marine On-Line (MOL) account to complete your Outbound Interview (OBI).
  • All Marines in receipt of PCS orders will utilize the OBI module within MOL. This covers all information needed by IPAC/admin to finalize PCS orders and will determine your travel entitlements. The OBI is used to finalize the right housing process. Be sure to include any pet(s) in your OBI.
Submit your Pet Information to PTO (Army as soon as you know your general departure month)
"Only Cats & Dogs are considered pets per the purposes of the AMC-PE. You assume all risk associated with the transportation of your pet(s)." Contact your local PTO immediately, as applicable, if you plan to move with a pet.
  • Name of member traveling with pet, estimated travel window, type of pet, dimensions of crate, weight of pet, & total weight of crate/carrier. If you plan to PCS with a pet(s), this should be done as soon as you know your general departure month.
  • Pet reservations will be apportioned on a first come/first serve basis. Space is limited on the AMC-PE, and there is no guarantee of a reservation no matter how early the process is initiated.
  • As of 10 March 2026, all service members must sign and submit to PTO the AMC Risk Management Document prior to receiving a pet spot on AMC. Ref 50
  • See 2026 Peak Season MARADMIN Section 8 on Transporting Pets. AMC-PE should always be the first option when transporting your pet. Visit the AMC Pet Travel Page for details on breed restrictions, kennel/carrier requirements, and pricing. Other options for moving pets include commercial airlines and pet relocation (3rd party shipping). These options can be more costly. Ref 48
  • Pet reimbursement is now authorized up to $2,000 for one pet: Ref 51 and Ref 52
  • Things to consider: If you are coming from a non-USMC location, the booking process may differ significantly. Contact your local PTO office as early as possible to learn their specific rules.
  • Air Mobility Command-Patriot Express (AMC-PE): DoD policy may allow passengers traveling in a PCS situation to ship pets at their own expense. Pets are limited to dogs and cats only, with no more than 2 pets per family and must be accompanied by their owner. The transportation of pets is also authorized in the baggage compartment (aircraft belly). Please remember AMC-PE flights may not be available when you rotate back from an overseas location, including overseas locations (and commercial options, shipping). Ref 53
  • As of May 1, 2026: If commercial travel is authorized, active duty and dependents may use a foreign flag carrier when on (12 flag carrier) able to provide pet transportation, and JTR Section 050107 B.2. Example: Flying JAL/Korea Air with no codeshare with a US airline.
Initiate the pet importation process for entry to Guam (if applicable) (This process may take around 6 months to complete)
  • Make an appointment with your local Veterinarian (preferably a military Veterinary Treatment Facility (VTF) familiar with the process).
  • Review the Guide for importing dogs or cats into Guam Ref 54. This website lists all the requirements necessary to import your pet. Take it with you to your veterinary appointment.
  • Reach out to Andersen AFB Pet Lodge to make your quarantine reservation. They will provide you with import requirements and contact the Guam Department of Agriculture. Ref 56
  • If flying commercial you may need to route through Hawaii and follow all of the Hawaii import regulations. Be sure to follow the 5 Day or Less Checklist Ref 55
Request a sponsor if you have not been assigned one
Contact the Sponsorship Coordinator (SC) of your gaining command or your local IR&R Office.
  • Per MCO 1320.11H Ref 2, sponsors are to be of equal or higher pay-grade to arriving service members. Commands are encouraged to match marital status. Ref 2
  • Your sponsor should be in contact with you no less than 60 days prior to arrival and will assist with housing, local resources, and more.
  • Your sponsor should receive Sponsorship Training (IR&R Office) in order to assist you.

III. The Earlier, the Better (180–90 Days Out)
Understand your HHG weight
It is crucial the service member understands the total weight of HHG they are authorized. Entitlements can be verified in the DTR IV "It's Your Move" on page 5. Use the PPA weight estimator tool Ref 25.
  • Pro Gear: does not count against your total weight allowance. Net weight limit is 2,000 lbs. Marines may request an additional 500 lbs for Spouse Pro Gear, which includes items required for employment and household materials. All Pro Gear requests MUST be submitted to the local PPO BEFORE the move.
  • Gun safes and armor: Must request at least 60 days in advance to 18,000 lbs. Must be shipped empty.
  • Tip: Gun safe requests are being denied with frequency. If denied, do not exceed 18,000 lbs.
Prepare to schedule your shipments
As soon as you receive your orders, start with DMO for HHG applications to lock in pack-out and pick-up dates.
  • Use the Installation Search Ref 5 option to find your nearest DMO (or Personal Property Office if not on a USMC base). Ref 24
  • If this is your first time moving, contact your local DMO first to receive instructions to create an account.
  • NEW (April 2026): Homeschooling materials may now be included as PBP&E (Pro Gear) — see JTR Appendix A.
Schedule your shipments in DPS (Unaccompanied Baggage (UB) Household NTS (Non-Temp Storage) Small Package PPM (Personally Procured))
Log into DPS Ref 26 to schedule your shipments. Each shipment will be submitted separately under the same order number. Have the following ready:
  • Contact info, estimated weight, pick-up & delivery locations and dates (no weekends or holidays), and any special items/PPG&E (Pro Gear).
  • Completing this step will generate your DD Forms. Review first, then sign. Contact your local DMO with any questions.
  • Small Package PPMs are not the primary method of moving HHG OCONUS. However, small-package shipments (e.g. via USPS) are authorized if your shipments combined weights do not exceed your entitled weight. These are small shipments (generally under 70 pounds, no more than 70 pounds per package) that the service member ships via USPS or other commercial couriers and are authorized for exact-cost reimbursement up to the standard shipping or insurance. The intent of Small Package PPMs is to enable the shipment of small household necessities (e.g. sheets, clothes) in an expedited manner to your destination. Review the USMC Handout on Small Package PPMs for more information. Ref 34
  • Accompanied Unaccompanied Baggage (UB) requires approval from your local PPO and receive approval from a PPO via signed DD 2278. Marines who fail to obtain PPO approval in advance of the move will not be paid an inconvenience
Start thinking about your vehicles: Will you ship or sell them
Visit PCSMyPOV Ref 35 to learn about POV (privately owned vehicle) shipping locations and to make an appointment for drop-off. You will need to notify DMO if you would like to ship a POV. Ref 31
  • Marines conducting a PCS move to Guam may be entitled to ship one POV at government expense. Contact your local DMO for information regarding POV shipment and to ensure required documents. Refer to "Shipping your POV" section in this document.
  • A second vehicle can be shipped at the member's own expense. See PCSMyPOV Ref 35 website above for more information.
  • Electric vehicles CAN NOT be shipped to or from Guam, see PCSMyPOV Ref 35 website above for more information.
Determine if you will apply for a passport
Guam is a U.S. territory, passports are not required for PCS travel. However, it is strongly recommended that eligible dependents obtain a valid passport and active duty obtain a tourist passport, in case of emergency travel (e.g., medical evacuation or leave requiring transit through a foreign country). Please refer to the "Passenger Companion Guide (PCG Clarified)."
Contact IPAC for Entitlement Questions
Know your entitlements — call or visit your local IPAC:
  • TLE (Temporary Lodging Expense): Authorized 7 days for CONUS-to-OCONUS moves. Ref 9
  • DLA (Dislocation Allowance): Partially reimburses for relocation expenses (typically reimbursed upon check-in once travel claim is completed). Ref 10
  • Per Diem: Flat rate for each day of travel. SM may not receive both per diem and TLE for the same day. Ref 11
  • TLA (Temporary Lodging Allowance): Authorized OCONUS 10 days at a time while waiting for permanent housing. Ref 12
  • OHA (Overseas Housing Allowance): If applying for off-base housing, you can use the calculator to find your OHA amount. Ref 13
  • GTCC: Never your limit. Ask your S-1/IODTA if you need to raise your limit. Ensure it is activated and in "mission-critical" status prior to departure.
  • LICWO: Contact your chain of command regarding this entitlement. Ref 14
Create a budget
Come up with a spending plan. There will be some up-front costs that will be reimbursed later, but also plan for start-up expenses once you arrive (pantry, cleaning supplies, utilities). Do not forget to request Dislocation Allowance (DLA) on your travel voucher once your PCS is complete. Advanced DLA is no longer authorized if the active duty Marine has a GTCC.
Communicate with your Sponsor
Provide your sponsor with the following:
  • A copy of your PCS orders
  • Your travel itinerary
  • Special Power of Attorney (SPOA) (optional). Granting the SPOA to your sponsor will allow them to do many things on your behalf before you arrive.
The key to a smooth PCS is communication with your sponsor and gaining unit.
Start sorting your Household items
Decide what you will ship, hand carry, and what you will purge. Homes are smaller in Guam, and storage upon arrival is limited.
  • Set aside items not authorized to be moved or stored. See Appendix A-1 "Hazardous Materials" — your TSP may have additional restrictions — discuss during your Pre-Move Questionnaire.
Renew your Driver's License (if applicable)
Check to ensure your driver's license will be valid for your whole tour in Guam. If your license expires while in Guam, you will be required to get a Guam license and the process can take over a year. Ask your sponsor for additional information.
  • Note: If using your driver's license to access a military installation or flying, a Real ID is required. Ref 15
Renew your Military ID (if applicable)
Dependents will need the active duty service member present to renew a military ID. Dependents without a Sponsor present must have a valid DD Form 1172-2 signed by the Sponsor and a Verifying Official (valid 90 days from signature). Power of Attorney accepted if it states DEERS or Military ID card related transactions. See Military One Source Ref 16 for potentially renewing online.

    These can be completed earlier but should be done prior to departure from old PDS.
    Complete pet import process for Guam

    Think about your cell phone plans

    The active duty Marine will begin the check-out process typically within 30 days of departure. Ensure you ask any last-minute questions regarding entitlements during this time.

    • Set aside suitcases (for travel), trash, and donations.

    Clean your home and ensure none of your personal belongings are left behind. Complete your home inspection (if applicable), turn in the keys, and say goodbye to your neighbors!

    VI. Prepping for Packout / Pickup
    Confirm dates with your TSP
    Contact your TSP to confirm all dates. Pack-out dates are the days leading up to your pick-up date (weight). The pick-up date is the day your belongings will be picked up up to the top of the truck.
    • Tip: TSP Pre-Move Survey completed. The assigned TSP will contact you to schedule — usually done at your home or over the phone. They will estimate weight and identify special items ordered in DPS.
    Complete your Pre-Move Survey
    Call your TSP or DMO if you are within one week of moving day and have not had a Pre-Move Survey completed. The assigned TSP will contact you to schedule — usually done at your home or over the phone. They will estimate weight and identify special items ordered in DPS.
    Read Tips from Military One Source & PPA
    Read the Military One Source Ref 39 & PPA: Moving and Packing Tips Ref 41
    Organize and separate shipments
    • Have separate rooms for each shipment keeps items organized so items are not misplaced in the wrong shipment.
    • TSPs may have their own policies surrounding spices, sealed foods, and other consumable items.
    • Drain lawnmower, tape remotes to electronics, remove all batteries, remove all items from walls (curtains, pictures, TVs), disassemble outdoor playsets, move items out of attic/crawl space, and ensure all appliances are disconnected.
    • Shipping alcohol is authorized per MARADMIN 289/19. Documentation is required to be submitted in advance. Please inform your move counselor when you want to ship alcohol to receive the proper paperwork. Ref 32
    • Lithium-Ion Batteries (as of May 2023): Restricted to <100Wh per battery & labeled separately. See Factsheet Ref 25
    • Create your own inventory: Start with your highest-value items. Keep all receipts, get appraisal records, and take photos/videos prior to packing. Video record electronics/appliances in working condition and photograph serial number plates. Ref 33
    • Keep a copy of your inventory in your move binder.

    Finalize housing
    If housing is not finalized before arrival, work with your sponsor to finalize housing requirements to be assigned housing.
    Complete Inbound Interview (IBI) and Travel Voucher NLT 5 days after arrival
    This will update your pay and entitlements. Ensure you submit any reimbursable receipts (pet fees, TLE, etc.) and select yes to receiving Dislocation Allowance (DLA). TLE may require a supplemental voucher depending on length of stay.
    Finalize with pay and entitlement payouts
    Check weekly on MOL travel tab for status updates on the travel voucher. Ensure all entitlements were paid at the correct rate.
    Welcome Aboard Brief
    Attend the welcome aboard brief if you signed up to attend.
    Familiarize yourself with your base
    Utilize the many resources on base such as Outdoor Recreation, Military Family Readiness Center, Andersen AFB, Fleet and Family Services (Navy Base Guam), and more. Orient yourself to the Commissary, Exchange, Post Office, and restaurants. MWR Camp Blaz Ref 59 will occasionally host tours for families. Contact your sponsor for additional information.
    Transition from TLF to your permanent residence
    If you have several pieces of luggage, ask your sponsor or unit to assist with moving your belongings into your new home.
    Receive your Personal Property Shipments (HHG)
    Communicate which items you would like the delivery crew to unpack. You have a couple of options, and you should communicate your personal choice with the TSP and add it in DPS Counseling "FULL UN-PACK REQUIRED"
    • 1) A full unpack: Movers empty all boxes into specified rooms, on a flat surface. They are not required to organize or put items away. If you would like it all unpacked, you need to indicate that in the remarks section in DPS Counseling. "FULL UN-PACK REQUIRED"
    • 2) A partial unpack: Movers only open boxes in select areas.
    • 3) Movers assemble furniture that they took apart at the origin (beds, couches, etc.)
    • If you refuse a full unpack, the TSP is not obligated to return to remove packing materials.
    • Your TSP should notify you directly when your shipments are ready for delivery (via email). Know the Required Delivery Date (RDD) which can be found in DPS. Check off on the "What to do when the moving company arrives" section in Military Disclosure. Ref 40
    • Should mold be discovered at ANY point during delivery, HALT DELIVERY. All items must be inspected and all items inspected prior to allowing movers to continue unloading. Use the inventory sheet to check off the numbers and ensure that everything is delivered. If those items need to be annotated on the inventory sheet before you sign off by the driver.
    Submit a Loss / Damage Report
    See PPA: The Claims Process Ref 42 for a full explanation of the claims process from start to finish.
    • Note: A Loss/Damage Report is different from an actual claim. You will still need to file a claim for lost or damaged items.
    • Complete DD Form 1840 with your TSP at the time of delivery if any damage is seen at delivery. If any HHG are lost or damaged, you are entitled to full replacement value (FRV).
    • 1) Inspect all items.
    • 2) Note obvious loss/damages and ask the driver to complete and sign DD Form 1840.
    • 3) Acknowledge receipt of all high-value items.
    • 4) Review all inventories and forms before signing.
    • You can also submit a Loss/Damage report AFTER DELIVERY. For shipments picked up on or before May 14, 2026: 9-month filing period. For shipments on or after May 15, 2026: 12-month filing period.
    • Questions? Contact the Navy Personnel Claims Unit at (888) 897-8217, (757) 440-6315, or navpersonnelclaims@navy.mil. Remember: this is a negotiation. If you are not happy with an offer, you should reject it.
    File an itemized claim for Loss / Damage
    Log in to DPS to submit a Claim for Loss/Damage. A Loss/Damage Report is NOT a claim — you must submit a separate itemized claim in DPS for every item lost or damaged.
    • Gather before submitting: Manufacturer, brief description, inventory item number, cost at purchase and year, description of damage, proof of value/condition, photos & receipts (if available).
    • For quick reference throughout the process, please refer to the claims factsheet Ref 25
    • Note: For real property damage, you will submit this type of claim directly to the TSP.
    • Questions? Contact the Navy Personnel Claims Unit at (888) 897-8217, (757) 440-6315, or navpersonnelclaims@navy.mil.
    • Remember: this is a negotiation. If you are not happy with an offer, you should reject it.
    File an Inconvenience Claim
    Contact your TSP to file an Inconvenience Claim if HHG are not picked up/delivered by the Required Delivery Date (RDD).
    • NEW for 2026: Reimbursement is now 100% of the MAIF per day for moves picked up on or after May 15, 2026; all dependents traveling on approved funded relocation orders are also authorized reimbursement at 75% of MAIF.
    Complete the Customer Satisfaction Survey (CSS)
    Complete the CSS for EACH shipment executed during the PCS. The CSS consists of eight questions and it will allow you to rate your TSP, HCMC receives all ratings and holds moving companies accountable. See PPA Factsheet Ref 25
    Finally, enjoy all that Guam has to offer
    Research local villages, language classes, cultural events, restaurants, and more. You put a lot of work into your move, now take some time to enjoy your new duty station overseas! For events and outings on check out MWR Andersen and Fleet and Family Services (Navy Base Guam). Both bases host monthly tours of the island.
